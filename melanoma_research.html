What is Melanoma?

Melanoma is the most dangerous form of skin cancer, responsible for approximately 75% of all skin cancer deaths despite representing only a small fraction of cases. Early detection is critical.

📈

Survival Rate: Early vs Late

When caught at Stage I, the five-year survival rate exceeds 99%. At Stage IV, that figure drops below 30%. This dramatic difference makes early screening one of the most impactful interventions in oncology.

🔍

Why AI Screening Matters

Even experienced dermatologists show significant inter-observer variability on borderline cases. AI models trained on large dermoscopy datasets provide consistent, objective second opinions to support clinical decision-making.

🌍

Global Incidence

Over 325,000 new melanoma cases are diagnosed globally each year. Populations with limited access to specialist dermatologists stand to benefit most from accessible, automated screening tools.

The ABCDE Rule of Melanoma

Dermatologists use the ABCDE criteria to assess suspicious lesions. Our model learns to detect these same visual patterns automatically from dermoscopy images.

A
Asymmetry
One half of the mole does not match the other half in shape or color.
B
Border
Edges are irregular, ragged, notched, or blurred rather than smooth.
C
Color
Uneven distribution — multiple shades of brown, black, red, or white.
D
Diameter
Larger than 6mm (about the size of a pencil eraser) or growing in size.

📄 Research Paper

This application is the working implementation of a published preprint. The paper validates a two-stage fine-tuning strategy that outperforms single-stage fine-tuning by 4.21% in sensitivity.

Two-Stage Fine-Tuning of ResNet50 for High-Sensitivity Melanoma Detection on Dermoscopic Images

Aryan Bhagat · MS Computer Science, Florida Atlantic University · ORCID: 0009-0006-0508-791X

This paper proposes a two-stage fine-tuning approach for ResNet50 applied to binary melanoma classification on dermoscopic images. Stage 1 trains only the classification head with the base frozen to stabilize randomly initialized weights. Stage 2 fine-tunes all layers jointly at lr=1e-5 to prevent catastrophic forgetting. An ablation study confirms the two-stage protocol outperforms single-stage fine-tuning with a 4.21% sensitivity gain. The model achieves 89.13% specificity — outperforming the published ASFF-ResNet50 benchmark on this clinically critical metric.

How the Model Works

The two-stage training protocol is the core research contribution of this paper.

🧊

Stage 1 — Head Training

All ResNet50 base layers are frozen. Only the new classification head trains for 15 epochs at lr=1e-3. This stabilizes randomly initialized weights before touching pretrained ImageNet features.

🔥

Stage 2 — Full Fine-Tuning

All layers are unfrozen and trained jointly at lr=1e-5. This carefully adapts ImageNet features to dermoscopic patterns without catastrophic forgetting of low-level visual features.

🎯

Preprocessing Alignment

Both training and inference use identical ResNet50 preprocess_input(). Many published models fail in deployment due to this mismatch — this paper explicitly addresses it.
